Dear Freelance Rails Dev,

Is the slog of client maintenance holding you back from reaching bigger and better business goals?

Do you get stuck between kicking off new, exciting projects and maintaining old ones?

You know that the work you need to do to launch new client projects isn’t the work that needs to be done to keep your clients happy forever.

You want to grow your business and you know that the best way to do that is to develop recurring revenue, upsell your clients on expanded services, and package your offerings to maximize value for your clients…

But where are you going to find the time?

How do you create the right kind of space in your work day to take things to the next level?

False starts on productized offerings?

Have you successfully sold clients on a maintenance package, only to get bogged down in a trickle of feature requests that keep you from making meaningful progress on other projects?

You know that it’s natural for software to grow and evolve once it’s out in the wild with your clients and their users…but how do you adapt to these natural changes when you do your best work in the flow?

You’re at your best heads-down, carving out core features of a new project, not responding to dribs and drabs of work that randomly trickle in.

The worst is when your clients know you’re bouncing back and forth and they start to feel like they’re only getting your “left-over hours.“ But how else are you supposed to create a solid foundation of recurring revenue AND break ground on new projects?

Tried outsourcing in the past?

It’s extremely difficult finding developers who know what they are doing. And when you do find them, how many want just a day or two a week of work? More likely, they want full time work, or they go off and build their own thing.

As a developer who cares about code quaility, it kills you to watch a hack of a sub-contractor fumble week after week. You could have gotten the work done yourself, better and faster, without wasting time trying to “manage” them.

Try again, with a professional.

If you really want to be the go-to dev for your clients, breaking ground on new, exciting projects, while keeping them happy in the long run AND shore up your own business with reliable recurring revenue in the form of ongoing support and maintenance offerings, you need a professional developer who can:

  • chew through bug reports and tiny code tweaks
  • knock out the small features your clients that will keep your clients happy
  • make progress on your backlog without constant supervision
  • know the right questions to ask you at the right time, when your input is needed
  • be available just enough to support a regular roll-up of client requests, without putting pressure on you to fill their plate with full-time work

Do you want to head in this direction with your freelance business? Then we should talk.
