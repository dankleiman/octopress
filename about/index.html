About

I really, really enjoy coding 9-5 every day at TrialNetworks. We’re helping companies get their drugs to people who need them faster and more efficiently by bringing technology solutions to clinical trials.

But I’m also looking for part-time (10-15 hours/week) freelance work.

What if I could:

  • Clear nagging exceptions out of your inbox by killing bugs for you?
  • Delight your clients with new features that are too small to show up on your radar?
  • Handle boring maintenance tasks, so you can free up your creative energy to focus on new projects?

Why not focus on the most gratifying, challenging parts of your freelance work and let me handle the mundane stuff that needs to get done, but isn’t where you can really shine?

Why am I interested in the boring stuff? It’s not boring to me. I’m on my first big climb of the learning curve. Every time I get dropped into a new code base, there are details, examples, and patterns that help me solidify the basics and show me new possibilities for style, composition, and design.

Selfishly, I want to learn from your experience. In exchange, I’ll kill the bugs, finish the chores, and build out the little things that will give your work a little more polish and make your users and clients a little happier.

Sound like a good trade? Get in touch, I’d love to talk more.

Email me: info at dankleiman.com

More About My Background

I am a recent graduate of Launch Academy in Boston. You’ll find posts in the archives all about my experiences learning at Launch (it was life-changing!).

Prior to Launch Academy, I ran Brookline Tai Chi for almost 10 years. I taught adults how to relax, slowdown, and improve their health with the Chinese movement arts of Tai Chi and qigong. In addition to being on of the lead instructors, I managed the school and the larger 501©(3) non-profit that runs the school.

Over the last 4 years, I expanded our Tai Chi for Healthy Aging program to bring Tai Chi to hundreds of seniors throughout Massachusetts. With funding from the Tufts Health Plan Foundation and the Department of Public Health and a close working partnership with the Massachusetts Falls Prevention Coalition, we’ve been able to train volunteer instructors in an evidence-based falls prevention program that is taught in Senior Centers, Councils on Aging, and other community settings.
